# Noise Reduction

Raw sEMG captures electrical potentials generated by muscle fibers during contraction. For an 8-sensor topology,
the hardware streams 16 specific directional vectors (accounting for positive/negative physiological phases).
Effective software-defined filtering is mandatory prior to classification:

- **Hardware-Level Filtering**: Analog front-end amplification paired with anti-aliasing low-pass filters to conform to
  the Nyquist-Shannon sampling limit before analog-to-digital conversion.
- **Digital Bandpass Filters**: Attenuates artifacts outside voluntary muscle activation ranges. A digital Butterworth
  filter isolates frequencies between 20 Hz and 450 Hz.
- **Notch Filters (50Hz / 60Hz)**: A highly narrow band-stop filter explicitly deployed to mitigate electrical grid power-
  line hum without distorting surrounding signal frequencies.
